### **Regional Language Variations**

<details>
<summary class="custom-summary rose">🌸 Cantonese Regions (Hong Kong, Macau, Guangdong)</summary>

<div class="region-card">
**Reality Check:** Completely different from Mandarin - like Italian vs Spanish!

**Key Phrase to Learn:**
- <span class="chinese-phrase">你好嗎？</span> <span class="pinyin">(nei hou ma?)</span> <span class="translation">"How are you?" in Cantonese</span>

**Strategy:** Use Mandarin + English in Hong Kong/Macau - most people understand both.
</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary class="custom-summary green">🏙️ Shanghainese (Shanghai Region)</summary>

<div class="region-card">
**Reality Check:** Local identity language, but everyone speaks Mandarin.

**Key Phrase to Learn:**
- <span class="chinese-phrase">侬好</span> <span class="pinyin">(nong hao)</span> <span class="translation">"Hello" in Shanghainese</span>

**Strategy:** Mandarin works perfectly everywhere in Shanghai.
</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary class="custom-summary orange">🌶️ Sichuanese (Sichuan, Chongqing)</summary>

<div class="region-card">
**Reality Check:** Mandarin with a thick accent and some local expressions.

**Key Phrase to Learn:**
- <span class="chinese-phrase">要得</span> <span class="pinyin">(yao de)</span> <span class="translation">"Good/OK" in Sichuanese</span>

**Strategy:** Standard Mandarin still works everywhere.
</div>
</details>

### **Advanced Language Tips**

<details>
<summary class="custom-summary blue">🧠 Pronunciation Secrets</summary>

<div class="region-card">
**Tone Change Rules:**
- <span class="chinese-phrase">你好</span> is actually pronounced <span class="pinyin">"ní hǎo"</span> (not "nǐ hǎo") due to tone sandhi
- Two 3rd tones together: first becomes 2nd tone
- Practice with: <span class="chinese-phrase">很好</span> <span class="pinyin">(hén hǎo)</span> <span class="translation">"very good"</span>
</div>
</details>

<details>
<summary class="custom-summary lime">🎯 Context Clues</summary>

<div class="region-card">
**Reading the Situation:**
- Hand gestures often accompany speech - watch for pointing and counting
- Facial expressions are universal - smile when confused!
- Business cards are always received with two hands
</div>
</details>
