<details>
<summary className="custom-summary green">
<span>🌱 Vegetarian & Vegan Navigation</span>
</summary>

China has a rich Buddhist vegetarian tradition with over 3,000 dedicated vegetarian restaurants nationwide. However, navigating vegetarian options requires knowledge of potential pitfalls.

### Key Vocabulary

<div className="payment-table-container">
| Chinese | Pinyin | English | When to Use |
|---------|--------|---------|-------------|
| <span className="chinese-phrase">素食</span> | <span className="pinyin">sùshí</span> | <span className="translation">Vegetarian</span> | General dietary preference |
| <span className="chinese-phrase">纯素</span> | <span className="pinyin">chún sù</span> | <span className="translation">Vegan</span> | Strict plant-based diet |
| <span className="chinese-phrase">素菜</span> | <span className="pinyin">sùcài</span> | <span className="translation">Vegetarian dishes</span> | Ordering from menu |
| <span className="chinese-phrase">全素</span> | <span className="pinyin">quán sù</span> | <span className="translation">Completely vegetarian</span> | Emphasizing no animal products |
</div>

:::warning Common Pitfalls
**Be Aware Of:**
- Vegetable dishes often cooked in lard (<span className="chinese-phrase">猪油</span>)
- Broths frequently contain meat or fish stock
- "No meat visible" doesn't guarantee vegetarian preparation
- Buddhist vegetarian excludes garlic, onions, and other "pungent" vegetables
:::

<span>⚠️ Food Allergy Management</span>
</summary>

Managing food allergies in China requires vigilance and clear communication, as cultural understanding of severe allergies may be limited.

### Critical Communication Strategy

**The Golden Rule**: Be direct and emphasize medical severity

**Key Phrase Structure**:
- Use <span className="chinese-phrase">过敏</span> (<span className="pinyin">guòmǐn</span>) for "allergy"
- Emphasize consequences: "If I eat [allergen], I will go to the hospital"
- <span className="chinese-phrase">如果我吃 [过敏源]，我就会去医院</span>
